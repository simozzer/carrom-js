How to play & rules

Aim of the game

You are White, the computer is Black (untick “computer vs computer” to watch it play itself). Flick the striker to pocket all nine of your own carrom men. First to 25 points, or the higher score after 8 boards, wins the match.

Controls

  • Position: drag the striker along your baseline (it can’t overlap a coin).
  • Aim & power: press and hold the board, then drag to set the direction. The power gauge oscillates up and down — release at the strength you want (release at zero to cancel).
  • Fine-tune: after releasing, the shot locks — nudge the angle with ← / → (hold to sweep faster), then Enter to fire or Esc to cancel.
  • Trajectories menu previews where the shot goes (None / first 2 collisions / full); Difficulty sets how precisely the computer plays.

Rules

  • Keep your turn by pocketing one of your own men; miss and the turn passes.
  • The Queen (red) must be covered: pocket one of your own men on the same or next stroke, or she’s returned to the centre. A covered Queen is worth 3 points.
  • Pocketing the striker is a foul — a potted coin is returned (or owed as a “due” if you have none), and your turn ends. Three fouls in a row costs an extra coin.
  • Pocketing an opponent’s coin simply gives it to them.
  • Scoring a board: the winner scores 1 point per opponent coin still on the board, plus 3 for a covered Queen.
